# Strategic pivot — from enterprise to fund-level

*This article documents the deliberate strategic shift in RbD's positioning that occurred during the Leanne AI brand strategy session. It is important context for understanding why certain language is used and why certain audiences are explicitly excluded.*

---

## The before state

Prior positioning targeted **Heads of Product** and **Fortune 1000 product leaders** — enterprise orientation. The language, taglines, and messaging were written for that audience.

*Example of old language:* "Most product leaders don't have a launch problem — they have a revenue alignment problem."

This was enterprise-facing. It did not speak to funds, boards, or capital allocators.

## The pivot decision

During the brand strategy session, Leanne AI identified a fundamental tension: RbD had three power narratives competing — enterprise product authority, fund-level capital discipline, and agency execution. They were powerful individually but not unified under a dominant category.

The question was posed: **"Who is your true apex buyer?"**

Elissa's answer, verbatim:

> "You're absolutely correct to call that out, and I am 100% on board with you. In the past, I was targeting product leaders and heads of product that signals enterprise orientation, and I have pivoted. I'm no longer targeting those folks. I am targeting fund-level standard, and that is my category. So, yes, are we clear?"

**That is the pivot. It is on record. It is intentional.**

## What changed

**No longer targeting:**
- Heads of Product
- Fortune 1000 product teams
- Enterprise CPOs

**Now targeting:**
- Series A VC funds investing in AI SaaS
- Venture-backed Series A–B founders already funded
- VC General Partners and Investment Committees

## The strategic rationale (documented in session)

Enterprise work scales by client count.
Fund-level infrastructure scales by portfolio count.

Enterprise credibility helps.
**Fund-level governance creates category ownership.**

The fund hires Elissa. The portfolio trusts her. Not a logo. Not a company. Her.

## The current positioning (post-pivot)

> "I implement a fund-level profitability governance standard for Series A AI SaaS portfolios — forcing revenue validation, capital discipline, and assumption-to-cash alignment before scale."

## What this means for messaging

- **Stop saying:** "I help Heads of Product…"
- **Start saying:** "I install a capital discipline standard across venture portfolios that forces validated revenue before scale."
- Enterprise credentials are referenced as **proof of operator depth**, not as target audience positioning
- The enterprise work done at APG is historical context and proof — not the current positioning category

## The brand architecture that emerged

**Category:** Fund-level capital discipline standard
**Mechanism:** The Profitable Method™ as a governance engine
**Delivery surface:** Venture-backed founders (they are the companies, not the primary buyer)
**Primary buyer:** The fund / GP

The agency layer (Revenue Growth Partners) is downstream leverage — not the identity.

## Why this matters for the wiki

Every article should reflect this pivot. If language targets "Heads of Product" or "enterprise product teams," it is outdated and should be updated. The current target is **venture-backed founders and VC GPs** — the fund-level standard is the positioning, and everything else ladders up to it.
