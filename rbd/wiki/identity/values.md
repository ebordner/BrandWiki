# Core values and beliefs

## The seven core beliefs (use verbatim in messaging)

These are not values statements — they are convictions that show up in every piece of content, every sales conversation, every client engagement:

1. **95% of startups fail.**
2. **The Top 5% build differently.**
3. **AI accelerates everything — including structural mistakes.**
4. **MVP alone is insufficient.**
5. **Revenue must be validated before scale.** (MVP+R)
6. **Profit is a design decision.**
7. **Structural alignment determines survival.**

## What RbD stands for (and against)

**Stands for:**
- Structure over speed
- Architecture over tactics
- Durability over traction
- Designing revenue before scale
- Operator-level accountability (not advisory distance)

**Stands against:**
- Hype cycles and AI theater
- MVP-only approaches that skip revenue validation
- Growth theater — revenue events mistaken for revenue architecture
- Agencies that execute without understanding the business model
- Consultants who advise without operating accountability

## Decision filters (what these values look like in practice)

- **Who RbD works with:** Founders who believe structure determines survival. GPs who want portfolio durability, not just portfolio growth.
- **Who RbD does not work with:** Founders chasing valuation optics. Funds underwriting hype cycles. Anyone who believes speed alone wins.
- **How RbD prices:** Reflects the value of structural clarity, not hours logged.
- **How RbD communicates:** Diagnostic, not motivational. Calm, not urgent. Institutional, not salesy.
