# Origin story

## 1999: The beginning

Elissa started her product management career in 1999 — before "product management" was a buzzword, before it was taught in universities, before there were CPOs. She circled a job listing in a newspaper for a Product Manager at a universal remote-control manufacturer in Cypress, California, with 25% global market share in its category.

Her interviewer: Camille Jayne, the highest-paid CEO in Orange County at the time, a former TCI executive who had reported directly to John Malone ("the cable cowboy"), and before that a Procter & Gamble executive. Elissa got the job straight out of college. That mentor set the trajectory for everything that followed.

## Twenty-five years of compounding playbooks

Over 25 years in Fortune 500 product leadership across OEM, consumer electronics, telecommunications, and wireless technology, Elissa brought products to market and with each launch refined the playbook further:

| Product | Year-one revenue |
|---|---|
| First private-label universal remote, Latin America | $3M |
| High-speed internet usage-based billing platform | $65M |
| Panoramic WiFi (in-home Wi-Fi) | $100M |

**Career total: close to $1 billion** in launch-driven revenue.

## The Panoramic WiFi story — where The Profitable Method™ was born

Before Elissa took over the in-home Wi-Fi product, a peer had already launched it — and sold **13 units at $129** to a base of 4 million high-speed internet customers. Her job: rebrand and relaunch what was essentially "air." To consumers, Wi-Fi was just "something in the air that was supposed to work flawlessly."

It took three full years:
- **Year 1:** Concept phase — market research, service design, financial model validation
- **Year 2:** Development — product and service blueprint built simultaneously
- **Year 3:** Market testing, iteration, national launch

The national launch opened on **Super Bowl Sunday, 2017** in San Diego with a Super Bowl commercial during the game. Result: **1 million customers and $100M in year one.** The product still generates monthly revenue today.

It was during this project — applying service design principles to the product development timeline alongside the customer experience team — that Elissa identified what she calls "the secret sauce": building the service experience alongside the product, not after it. That discovery became the heart of The Profitable Method™.

## Why starting from failure matters

The Wi-Fi product that became $100M had already failed before Elissa touched it. That context is central to the positioning: she didn't build something new from scratch. She inherited a broken launch, diagnosed it, and rebuilt it using the full method. This is what she does for founders and GPs — diagnose structural failure points and engineer them out before scale magnifies them.

## The move to startups

After her corporate career, Elissa founded Atlanta Product Group to bring Fortune 500 methodology to the startup world. Her reasoning: startup founders face the exact same failure patterns as corporate product managers — without the institutional support, experience, or safety net.

The CB Insights postmortem of 110+ startup failures confirmed it: the top 12 reasons startups fail are the same structural problems she had spent 25 years engineering out of Fortune 500 product launches.

## The evolution to Revenue by Design

Atlanta Product Group was the original container. Revenue by Design is the intentional evolution — a brand name that embeds the core philosophy: you don't discover revenue. You design it.

*Profit is a design decision.*
