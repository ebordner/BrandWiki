## What to listen for when ingesting sales calls

**Surface objection → real concern underneath → what works**

**Categories to capture:**
- Price / budget — "It's a lot" / "I need to think about the investment"
- Timing — "Not the right time" / "After our next round"
- Certainty — "I'm not sure this applies to my stage"
- Authority — "I need to run this by my co-founder / board"
- "We're already doing this" — conflating RbD with product management or RevOps

## Known signals from existing source material

From the Leanne AI thread, the founder's own hesitation before outreach was "cosmetic alignment" — not pricing, not authority, not imposter syndrome. The pattern: the right buyer's objections are often cosmetic, not structural. When someone raises structural objections (not the right audience, wrong stage), they're probably a disqualifier, not an objection to handle.
