# Revenue Architecture Diagnostic — $222

## What it is

An async diagnostic tool. The buyer submits structured information about their company — product, revenue model, tech stack, customer journey, pricing — and receives a **personalized written Revenue Architecture Snapshot** back.

Not a course. Not a video. Not a live session.

They do the work (60–90 minutes of structured submission). Elissa's methodology processes it. They get a specific, personalized report showing exactly where their architecture is exposed.

---

## How it works

1. **Buyer purchases** and receives a structured intake form
2. **Buyer completes the intake** — 60–90 minutes of structured input covering their product, financial model, tech stack, customer journey, pricing assumptions, and growth stage
3. **Elissa (or methodology-informed process) reviews** and maps responses against the 10 structural failure points and MVP+R™ framework
4. **Buyer receives their Revenue Architecture Snapshot** — a personalized written report identifying structural exposures, misalignments, and priority areas to address

---

## The deliverable: Revenue Architecture Snapshot

A personalized written report covering:

- **Structural exposure map** — where their product, pricing, tech stack, and customer journey are misaligned
- **Revenue logic assessment** — where their monetization assumptions are weak or unvalidated
- **Top 3 priority flags** — the highest-risk structural failure points for their specific situation
- **AI compression vulnerability** — where features or moats could be eroded in 60–90 days
- **Next step recommendation** — whether they're ready to self-implement, need the full $5K Revenue Architecture Audit, or need a specific framework from the methodology

## Who it's for

- Founders who've consumed the $27 or $47 content and want to know *specifically* where they stand
- GPs who want a structural read on a portfolio company before deploying more capital — without a full engagement
- Founders preparing for a funding round who want their revenue logic stress-tested before the pitch
- Anyone who suspects structural exposure but isn't ready to commit to the full $5K Audit

**The self-selection filter:** The 60–90 minute intake is not passive. Buyers who complete it are signaling real commitment. Low-quality buyers self-select out.

## Ascension trigger

The Revenue Architecture Snapshot report should include a clear next step section. If the diagnostic reveals significant structural exposure (which it almost always will at Series A/B stage), the natural conclusion is:

*"Based on what we've mapped here, a Revenue Architecture Audit would address [specific exposures identified]. Here's how to apply."*

This makes the $5K CTA feel diagnostic, not salesy — it flows directly from what the report revealed.

## Operational notes

**Intake form design:** The form should map directly to the 10 structural failure points and the MVP+R™ framework layers (financial model, service design, tech stack, customer journey, market validation). Questions should surface the information needed to identify the most common collapse patterns.

**Turnaround:** 48-hour report delivery is a reasonable target. Creates urgency without creating operational bottleneck.

**Scalability:** Because it's async, this can be systematized. The intake form + report template can be partially systematized using the framework, making it the most scalable high-touch offer in the stack.

**AI assist opportunity:** This is a natural candidate for the Revenue by Design™ OS (future AI SaaS platform) — the intake form feeds the platform, which generates a structured snapshot, which Elissa reviews and adds insight to before delivery.
