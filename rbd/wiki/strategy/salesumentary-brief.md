# Salesumentary creative brief

*Creative brief for Jeremy Finlay's sales documentary production. Covers narrative architecture, psychological arc, tone, and all scene/line guidance developed during the Leanne AI session.*

---

## What a Salesumentary is (Jeremy Finlay's definition)

A long-form video asset (45–90 minutes) with documentary-grade production value, designed to run as paid cold or warm traffic (YouTube, Facebook, native ads) and convert at scale without webinars or VSLs. Core principles:

- **Story first, sell second** — viewer forgets they're being sold to
- **Documentary production quality** — real people, real locations, cinematic B-roll, professional scoring (HBO/Netflix feel, not infomercial)
- **Trojan Horse structure** — open with a compelling human problem → follow transformation journey → reveal the offer as the natural conclusion → CTA
- **Zero hype, maximum proof** — real testimonials, case studies, behind-the-scenes, third-party validation woven into the story
- **Evergreen + direct-response** — runs profitably on paid traffic at scale
- **Reported ROI** — $10M–$40M+ in sales for high-ticket offers; $1–$3M from $50K–$150K ad spend

---

## The big idea (opening frame)

> 95% of startups fail. Not because they lack ambition. Not because they lack funding. Not because they lack an MVP. They fail because scale exposes structural misalignment.

This documentary explores: *What separates the 95% from the 5%? And what does it actually take to build a company that holds under pressure?*

---

## Tone

**Think:** The Big Short meets startup boardroom.

Not: Tony Robbins. Not: webinar energy.

Feel: Institutional. Cinematic. Quietly dominant. Not aggressive. Not motivational. Elevated.

Specific calibration: **Calm + controlled + quietly dominant** (a blend of sharp + controlled).

---

## The 12 psychological sales gates (Jeremy Finlay's framework)

Each gate must "click" before the next, or the sale stalls. Structure: 70% build-up (gates 1–8), 20% proof/urgency (9–11), 10% close (12).

| Gate | Name | Psychological mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Awareness (Curiosity Hook) | Zeigarnik effect — unfinished thoughts linger |
| 2 | Empathy (Emotional Resonance) | Reciprocity — shared vulnerability |
| 3 | Validation (Social Proof Entry) | Bandwagon — others like me are transforming |
| 4 | Agitation (Pain Amplification) | Loss aversion — fear of inaction hurts more than gain |
| 5 | Hope (Glimpse of Possibility) | Anchoring — sets high-value expectation early |
| 6 | Doubt (Objection Neutralizer) | Honesty disarms skepticism — preempt with honest flaws |
| 7 | Aspiration (Identity Shift) | Self-concept — they buy to become the hero |
| 8 | Proof (Evidence Cascade) | Confirmation bias — stack "yes" moments |
| 9 | Urgency (Time Compression) | Scarcity — narrative momentum, not countdown timers |
| 10 | Simplicity (Path of Least Resistance) | Cognitive ease — simple over smart |
| 11 | Commitment (Micro-Yes Ladder) | Foot-in-the-door — "apply" not "buy" |
| 12 | Epiphany (Irreversible Buy) | Endowment effect — they mentally own the transformation |

**Pacing:** Each gate ~5–10 minutes, synced to emotional beats (music swells at hope, tension at agitation).

**Common pitfall:** Rushing gates 4–6. Agitation without hope feels manipulative and kills trust.

## Narrative architecture

### 1. The core human problem (Trojan Horse entry)

This is not a story about product. It is a story about **pressure.**

*Emotional hook:*
- The night before a board meeting
- The moment of writing the next check
- The quiet fear that growth is amplifying fragility

*Opening footage:* Founders in late-night work sessions. VCs in investment committee discussions. Revenue dashboards. Slack notifications. Headlines about startup collapse.

*Voiceover:* "95% of startups fail. But most of them were growing."

The audience leans in.

### 2. The identity conflict

**Founder conflict:** "I'm scaling fast. But does this actually hold at unicorn scale?"

**GP conflict:** "Am I underwriting growth — or underwriting fragility?"

*Positioning:* The 5% think differently. They engineer differently. They scale differently.

### 3. Introducing Elissa (without selling her)

Not a consultant. Not a growth strategist. But someone who has seen scale from both sides:
- Fortune 500 product systems
- Venture-backed startups under capital pressure
- Nearly $1B in launch-driven revenue
- Creator of The Profitable Method™ and MVP+R™

She is positioned as: the one who studies why 95% collapse — and systematically engineers those traps out before scale magnifies them.

**No pitch yet. Just perspective.**

### 4. The transformation journey (character arc)

Follow:
- A founder pre-alignment
- A VC reviewing portfolio risk
- The internal tension before structural clarity

Then we see: diagnosis of failure traps, revenue logic re-architected, monetization validated pre-scale, expansion pathways intentionally designed.

**We don't show "systems." We show: Relief. Clarity. Confidence. Calm.**

*Founder before:* Momentum with uncertainty. *After:* Scale with inevitability.

*GP before:* Growth with tension. *After:* Performance with discipline.

### 5. The proof layer (zero hype)

Proof is woven naturally:
- Panoramic WiFi → $100M year one
- Case studies of monetization alignment
- Real testimonials: "This changed how we build." / "This reduced risk before deployment."

No exaggerated claims. No "10x your revenue." Just: *Structural durability produces compounding outcomes.*

### 6. The philosophical reframe (midfilm)

> "Scale doesn't kill startups. Misalignment does."

> "AI accelerates everything. Including structural mistakes."

> "Momentum builds hype. Architecture builds valuation."

*This elevates the conversation from tactics to altitude thinking.*

### 7. The identity elevation (emotional climax)

Not revenue. **Identity.**

*Founder becomes:* Calm under pressure. Board-ready. Building for altitude. Part of the 5%.

*GP becomes:* Portfolio architect. Capital deployed with structural discipline. Known for durable performance.

This is the moment the viewer sees themselves in that role.

### 8. The offer reveal (natural conclusion)

Only after transformation and proof:

*"This is the work we do."*

Introduce: The Profitable Method™, MVP+R™, Revenue Expansion Architecture. Not as a product — as the mechanism behind the identity shift.

Then: *Invitation to apply. Not buy. Apply.* Because this is not for everyone.

## Narrative format recommendation

**Dual narrative (founder + GP interwoven)** — parallel stories that converge on the shared insight: structure determines who survives. Intercutting their moments of tension creates the strongest emotional arc and broadens addressable audience.

---

## Scroll-stopping language (approved for use in Salesumentary)

- "Most startups scale fast. 95% still fail."
- "Scale doesn't kill companies. Structural misalignment does."
- "Momentum impresses. Architecture survives."
- "AI accelerates everything. Including weak foundations."
- "You don't become a unicorn by moving faster. You become one by being structurally undeniable."
- "The difference between 95% and 5% isn't ambition. It's discipline under pressure."
- "If scale is coming, fragility is not an option."
- "Most founders build for growth. The top 5% build for survival at altitude."
- "Most funds allocate capital. The top 5% engineer durability inside their portfolio."

---

## Cold traffic ad entry point

> "95% of startups fail. Most of them were growing. Here's what the top 5% do differently."

This line will stop qualified founders and GPs.

---

## What makes this Salesumentary powerful

It combines:
- Statistical inevitability (95%)
- Institutional credibility
- Structural insight
- Identity elevation
- High-stakes narrative

And positions Elissa as: **The architect behind durable scale.**

---

## Strategic angle for Jeremy

The Salesumentary is not about: "How to grow."

It is about: **"How to avoid becoming part of the 95%."**

That tension alone carries the entire film.
