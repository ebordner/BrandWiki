## The 4 states of the buyer (critical framework)

Developed from Jeremy Finlay's methodology. The Salesumentary must meet buyers where they are and move them through all four states.

### State 1: Asleep
*Not ignorant — unaware of the real problem.*

**Founder asleep:** Believes "we need to execute harder" / "we need better marketing" / "we need more features" / "we need to ship faster." Does not yet see structural misalignment as the core issue. Sees symptoms. Misattributes the cause.

**GP asleep:** Believes "this is an AI cycle" / "moats are compressing" / "we need better founders." Does not yet see structural revenue architecture as the missing filter. Sees volatility. Misattributes the cause.

### State 2: Aware
*They know something is wrong.*

**Founder aware:** Noticing rework cycles, board pressure, monetization friction, growth not compounding. Doesn't know the solution but knows the symptoms aren't random.

**GP aware:** Noticing froth, fragility, AI compressing cycles, labs wiping companies out. Feels portfolio anxiety but doesn't yet see architecture as the solution.

### State 3: Awake
*They understand the real problem.*

**Founder realizes:** "It's not an execution issue. It's structural alignment."

**GP realizes:** "It's not an AI bubble issue. It's structural fragility underwriting."

This is where: the MVP myth breaks, the 95% statistic lands, the 10 failure points reframe the conversation, the AI-accelerates-cracks insight hits.

### State 4: Active
*They don't want another tactic, hire, or tool. They want structural durability, revenue architecture, top 5% identity. This is where the offer becomes inevitable.*

### Critical insight
**Your market is not asleep to growth. They are asleep to architecture.** That's the difference. The Salesumentary must mirror symptoms (State 2), agitate fragility (2→3), reframe the problem (State 3), present architecture as the identity bridge (State 4).

## 10-scene visual storyboard

*For Jeremy Finlay's pre-production. Tone: Institutional. Cinematic. Quietly dominant. The Big Short meets startup boardroom.*

**Scene 1 — The Statistic**
Visual: Black screen. Silence. White text fades in slowly: "95% of startups fail."
Cuts: News headlines of startup collapses → Slack notifications blinking → Empty coworking spaces → A founder alone at night
Voiceover (calm, steady): "95% of startups fail. Most of them were growing." Pause.

**Scene 2 — The Illusion of Growth**
Visual fast cuts: Revenue dashboard going up → Team celebrating funding round → Series A announcement → AI headlines everywhere
Voiceover: "They had funding. They had traction. They had an MVP." Cut to stillness. "They did not lack ambition. They lacked alignment." Music shifts — tension.

**Scene 3 — The Pressure Moment (Founder)**
Visual: Boardroom. Slide deck on screen. Investor asks a question. Close-up on founder's face.
Voiceover: "Scale doesn't kill companies. Misalignment does."
Cut to: Product roadmap → Financial model → Backend architecture diagram. None aligned. Subtle visual dissonance — layers not matching.

**Scene 4 — AI Acceleration**
Visual fast montage: AI tools launching → Code generating instantly → Competitor product announcement → Investor memo about "AI compression"
Voiceover: "AI accelerates everything. Distribution. Competition. Irrelevance. And structural cracks." Beat. "Speed is no longer an advantage. Structure is." Music becomes controlled, grounded.

**Scene 5 — Structural Engineering Metaphor**
Visual: Skyscraper under construction. Steel beams. Load-bearing columns. Architect reviewing blueprints. Cut to startup architecture diagrams layered over blueprint visuals.
Voiceover: "The companies that survive this decade will not be the fastest. They will be the most structurally sound."

**Scene 6 — Introduction of Elissa**
Visual: Elissa on camera explaining failure points. Clips of her breaking down: the 10 common collapse points, design sprints, flipping the financials, service design blueprint. Overlay keywords: ALIGNMENT / MONETIZATION / ARCHITECTURE / EXPANSION
Jeremy voiceover: "This is not about growth hacks. It's about eliminating the structural traps that collapse 95%."

**Scene 7 — The Founder Transformation**
Before: Late-night stress. Slack chaos. Reworking features.
After: Clean financial model. Board nodding. Revenue expansion slide.
Voiceover: "Profit is not luck. Valuation is not momentum. Profit is a design decision." Founder sits calm at head of table.

**Scene 8 — The VC Transformation**
Before: IC tension. "AI risk exposure" slide.
After: GP reviewing structurally aligned company. Confident nod.
Voiceover: "The top 5% don't underwrite hype cycles. They build durable portfolios."

**Scene 9 — Identity Elevation**
Montage: Clean architecture diagrams → Revenue expansion pathways → Service blueprint overlays → AI interface screens
Voiceover: "The question is not: 'How fast can you grow?' The question is: 'Will it hold?'" Pause.

**Scene 10 — Closing Identity Frame**
Black background. Slow, grounded music.
"If unicorn scale is the target, your architecture should already reflect it."
Fade in: REVENUE BY DESIGN / Profit is a design decision. Beat.
"The top 5% are not lucky. They are engineered."
Cut to application CTA.

**Music direction:** Minimalist. Hans Zimmer-style tension build. Low strings. Subtle percussion.
**Color palette:** Dark slate. Steel. Deep navy. Gold accents for revenue overlays.
**Camera style:** Slow dolly movements. Close facial shots. Wide boardroom frames. No handheld chaos.
**Energy:** Controlled dominance. Not hype. Not motivational. Institutional calm.

## 10-minute VSL outline (Revenue Architecture Audit)

*Jeremy Finlay style. Full timecodes.*

**0:00–0:45 — The Pattern (Cold Open)**
Visual: Boardroom silence. Founder alone at laptop. Slack notifications. Revenue dashboard.
VO: "95% of startups fail. Most of them were growing. Most of them had funding. Most of them had a product. Growth didn't kill them. Structural fragility did. [Pause.] AI is accelerating everything. Including structural cracks."

**0:45–2:00 — The Problem They Feel (Symptom Layer)**
Elissa on camera, mirroring language:
"If you're a venture-backed founder, you've probably felt this. You're growing. But things keep going back to the drawing board. Product is shipping — but margin isn't clean. CRM doesn't give you visibility. Investors ask questions you can't answer cleanly. Something feels off. But you can't pinpoint it." [Cut] "For GPs — it's different, but similar. Revenue is growing. But defensibility isn't clear. You're underwriting traction — but you're not sure you're underwriting durability." No selling yet. Just mirroring.

**2:00–3:30 — Reframe: It's Not Growth. It's Architecture.**
"The issue isn't marketing. It's not sales. It's not speed. It's revenue architecture."
Define cleanly: Revenue architecture is the alignment between what you promise the market, what you're actually building, how it's monetized, how the tech stack supports it, and whether the margin holds under scale.
"If those aren't aligned — scale compounds fragility."

**3:30–5:00 — Authority Layer (Subtle)**
"Over 20+ years in product leadership… Nearly $1B in launch-driven revenue… Venture-backed startups and Fortune 500 systems… I've seen the same structural failure patterns repeat." Then: "There are common collapse points. Most founders don't see them until scale exposes them." Clips of Elissa explaining the 10 failure points. Brief. Controlled. No hype.

**5:00–6:30 — AI Compression Reality**
"AI is compressing time to irrelevance. It is commoditizing features. It is collapsing distribution advantages. It is shrinking moats. [Pause.] In that environment, what survives isn't speed. It's structural alignment." Positions above trend talk.

**6:30–7:30 — Introduce the Revenue Architecture Audit**
"That's why I created the Revenue Architecture Audit."
Clear. Not hyped.
"It's a paid diagnostic engagement where we: Map your product to your revenue model. Align your tech stack with monetization logic. Stress-test margin under scale. Identify AI compression risk. And expose structural fragility before growth compounds it."

**7:30–8:45 — What They Walk Away With**
"Within two weeks, you receive: A documented Structural Exposure Brief. A margin durability assessment. A technology alignment diagram. A prioritized reinforcement roadmap. And a board-ready executive summary." This is where seriousness clicks.

**8:45–9:30 — Identity Filter**
"This is not for founders chasing valuation optics. It's for founders engineering enterprise value. This is not for funds underwriting hype cycles. It's for GPs building portfolios designed to outlast them. If you believe speed alone wins, this won't resonate. If you believe structure determines survival — you're in the right place." Controlled delivery.

**9:30–10:00 — CTA**
"If this resonates, the next step is to initiate a Revenue Architecture Audit. This is a paid engagement. It's designed to give you clarity before scale compounds risk. Click below to begin." No urgency timers. No manipulation. No hype. Authority close.

**Why this works — movement arc:**
Awareness (structural fragility) → Emotional resonance (I've felt this) → Reframe (architecture, not growth) → Authority (you've seen it) → Urgency (AI compression) → Solution (diagnostic) → Identity filter → Clean CTA
