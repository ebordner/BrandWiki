# The Profitable Method™

## What it is

A 10-step acrostic framework — **P-R-O-F-I-T-A-B-L-E** — representing the complete playbook for bringing a product to market in a way that addresses every major reason startups fail, as documented by CB Insights in their postmortem of 110+ startup failures.

Not another MVP-first approach. Validation does not stop at product/market fit. Every aspect of the business — financial model, service experience, revenue assumptions, technical architecture — is validated *before* development begins.

**Origin:** Refined over 25 years of Fortune 500 product launches. Applied in final form to Panoramic WiFi, which launched nationally in 2017 and generated $100M in year one, 1M customers.

**Published:** *The Profitable Startup* (Elissa Bordner, 2022, Difference Press).

---

## The CB Insights failure list this framework addresses

CB Insights analyzed 110+ startup postmortems and identified the top 12 failure reasons. The Profitable Method™ is purpose-built to address them all:

1. Run out of cash / failed to raise new capital
2. No market need
3. Got outcompeted
4. Flawed financial business model
5. Regulatory / legal challenges
6. Pricing / cost issues
7. Not the right team
8. Poor product — ignored customer needs
9. No product pivot when needed
10. Poor marketing
11. Lost investor / board focus
12. Failure to launch / scale

## The PROFITABLE Acrostic — Complete

### P — Perceive the Full Market Landscape, and Your Place In It

Deep market research: industry reports, trend analysis, competitive landscape mapping, market sizing.

**Why it matters:** Venture capital firms invest in experts. You must be an expert not just in your company and product, but in your entire industry. Most founders skip or under-invest here.

**Output:** Fully informed market position before a dollar is spent on development.

---

### R — Research with Empathy

Direct consumer research — talking with customers to uncover innovation opportunities, validate product/market fit, financial model assumptions, and the technology solution.

**Why it matters:** With deep consumer research, you don't just validate the product idea — you validate that customers will adopt and be *delighted* by what you build, and discover differentiation the market doesn't yet know it wants.

**Output:** Validated assumptions, differentiated market position, and certainty before development.

---

### O — Optimize Your Financial Model

Flipping the typical approach. Instead of: product requirements → financial model, the Profitable Method reverses it: **financial model → product requirements.**

**Why it matters:** Most startups build a business case that follows the product. This leaves massive revenue on the table. By letting the financial model *inform* the product requirements, you hunt for additional revenue opportunities from the start.

**This is the "Flipping the Financial Business Model" pillar.** Financial model must have validated assumptions — validated on a small scale with real customers — before development.

**Output:** Explosive revenue opportunities and a validated financial model.

---

### F — Facilitate Market and Technical Validation

Validating both market assumptions *and* the technical solution simultaneously, through a design sprint run in a real-world environment that simulates how the customer will use the product.

**Why it matters:** Most approaches validate one or the other. The Profitable Method validates both, in a simulated real-world context, *before* writing big development checks.

**Output:** Identification of potential issues before build. Market performance insights before investment.

---

### I — Improve Your Product Offering with a Service Strategy

Building a complete service design blueprint — mapping the end-to-end customer experience — alongside the product, not after it.

**Why it matters:** Anyone can build a product. Almost no one thinks about the *service experience* customers have throughout the customer journey. This is the differentiation layer that separates the top 5% from everyone else.

**Output:** Service design blueprint ready to execute on launch day. Customer delight from day one.

---

### T — Tie Together Requirements, Financial Model, and Technology Architecture

Complete alignment check — ensuring product requirements, financial model, and technical architecture are all consistent with each other before development begins.

**Why it matters:** Misalignment between these three layers is one of the most common and most expensive sources of startup failure. Gaps discovered in development cost exponentially more to fix.

**Output:** Ticked-and-tied foundation with zero costly surprises in development.

---

### A — Accelerate Product and Service Iterations Prior to Development

Running both the product solution *and* the service design together through a design sprint in a simulated real-world environment.

**Why it matters:** Surfaces blind spots in the comprehensive solution that other methods miss entirely. The design sprint at this stage is not for brainstorming — it's for stress-testing the complete solution before development dollars are committed.

**Output:** Stress-tested, iterated product-and-service solution with known blind spots resolved.

---

### B — Begin Development after a Gate Check

Formal gate review before entering development — confirming all business validation work is complete, all assumptions are validated, and the team is ready to build.

**Why it matters:** This is the defining discipline of The Profitable Method™. Rather than rushing into development at MVP, the method ensures that *all* aspects of the business surrounding the product have been validated — not just product/market fit. There are many reasons startups fail; product/market fit is only one.

**Output:** Development begins with confidence, not hope.

---

### L — Launch Development of Your Product and Service Blueprint

Development phase begins — but building both the product *and* the service experience simultaneously, according to the service blueprint.

**Why it matters:** Resources dedicated to building are building the *entire solution* — product + service — not just the product layer.

**Output:** Launch-ready product with a launch-ready service experience. Success out of the gate.

---

### E — Execute Your Product and Service Launch with Success

The go-to-market strategy — meticulous coordination and communication for the launch itself.

**Why it matters:** Most startups under-invest in launch planning. The Profitable Method treats launch as a discipline with its own rigor.

**Output:** An on-time launch that delights customers and investors from day one.

## The Profitable Method vs. Lean Startup

The Lean Startup (Eric Ries) and MVP methodology (Dan Olsen) are valuable tools. The Profitable Method is not anti-Lean — it extends and completes what Lean leaves unfinished.

**The Lean Startup's gap:** Validates engagement. Does not validate durability. Encourages rapid experimentation without a compelling strategy.

**What startup veterans say:**
- Ben Silbermann (Pinterest co-founder): "The hard part about 'minimum viable product,' for me, is you don't know what 'minimum' is, and you don't know what 'viable' is."
- Patrick Campbell (ProfitWell): "I think 'Lean Startup' and the MVP did more harm than good. Most end up shipping a piece of crap and going 'there's no market.'"
- Sachin Rekhi (Notejoy, ex-LinkedIn): followed Lean exactly, grew to 100,000 users, still failed to find a viable business model.

**The answer:** Validate product *and* revenue *and* service *and* financial model *and* technical architecture — all before development.

## MVP+R™ — The Revenue Validation Layer

**Full name:** Minimum Viable Product + Revenue

**Core principle:** Revenue must be validated simultaneously with product, not discovered after traction.

> "Revenue should not be discovered accidentally after traction. It should be designed intentionally before scale."

**What MVP+R™ validates:** Financial hypothesis, pricing logic, margin assumptions, revenue expansion pathways, operational feasibility, infrastructure alignment.

**MVP validates engagement. MVP+R™ validates durability.**

This concept is also the name and core content of the $27 entry-point offer.

## The three delivery pillars

### Design Sprints (Reengineered)
Pressure-tested workshop format for *decision velocity* — not brainstorming. Compresses months of friction into one focused session. Forces alignment, dissolves silos. Collapses revision cycles from 3 years to 3 days. Used at both the F (Facilitate) and A (Accelerate) steps.

### Flipping the Financial Model
The O (Optimize) step operationalized. Financial model drives product requirements — not the reverse. Every feature maps to margin logic. "If you can't trace a feature to margin, you're scaling fragility."

### Service Design Blueprints
The I (Improve) and L (Launch) steps operationalized. End-to-end mapping across: Learn → Buy → Get → Use → Pay → Support. Aligns marketing, sales, product, and ops into one integrated customer experience engine.
