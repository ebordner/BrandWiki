# Competitive landscape

## The competitive frame

Most advisors operate in silos. RbD integrates what they leave disconnected.

| Silo competitors | What they offer | What they miss |
|---|---|---|
| Product consultants | Product strategy and roadmap | Revenue architecture, financial model, service design |
| RevOps operators | Revenue operations and CRM | Product architecture, tech stack alignment |
| Pricing strategists | Pricing optimization | Full financial model, lifecycle mapping |
| Marketing agencies | Brand and demand generation | Structural revenue logic |
| Tech stack advisors | Technology architecture | Revenue model validation, product-margin alignment |
| AI governance consultants | AI policy and risk | Revenue impact, structural durability |

**No one else connects:**
> Product → Revenue → Technology → Lifecycle → AI compression → Enterprise value

That integration is the moat.

## The Lean Startup as competitive positioning

The dominant methodology in the startup world — MVP-first, validated learning, pivot — is the category RbD implicitly competes against. Not another firm, but an ingrained belief system.

**The positioning counter:**
- Lean Startup validates engagement. MVP+R™ validates durability.
- Lean Startup stops at product/market fit. The Profitable Method™ validates the entire business before development.
- Lean Startup was designed for early-stage uncertainty. The Profitable Method™ is designed for the structural rigor that capital scale demands.

Credentialed critics of MVP-only: Ben Silbermann (Pinterest), Patrick Campbell (ProfitWell), Sachin Rekhi (Notejoy / LinkedIn). See [The Profitable Method™](../frameworks/profitable-method.md) for full quotes.

## The alternatives clients consider

When a founder or GP considers RbD, they are typically choosing between:

1. **Doing nothing** — scaling with the architecture they have, hoping it holds
2. **Hiring in-house** — fractional CPO, Head of Product, CRO
3. **A traditional agency** — for the execution layer only
4. **General business coaching** — for strategy without structural specificity

RbD's answer to each:
- vs. doing nothing: "Scale magnifies structure. It does not fix it."
- vs. in-house: RbD brings 25+ years of cross-industry operator experience no hire can replicate in the near term
- vs. agency: Agencies execute. RbD architects first, then executes.
- vs. coaching: Coaching advises. RbD diagnoses and delivers.
